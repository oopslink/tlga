## Design Context

### Users
A child (primary user) who fills in daily progress — completes tasks, writes reflections, submits for approval.
A parent/guardian (secondary user) who reviews and approves the child's progress sheets.
This appears to be a custom family app, not a multi-tenant product.

### Brand Personality
Encouraging, gamified, warm. RPG adventure framing (gold coins, XP, levels, dungeons, achievements).
Three words: **playful, motivating, structured**.
The app rewards learning through a game layer — treat it like a kid's adventure logbook.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light theme, warm cream background (#fef3f0)
- Pink primary (#ff6b9d), gold (#ffb627), XP blue (#5eaeff), star purple (#c77dff)
- Chinese language UI throughout
- WeChat Mini Program (uni-app) — no hover states, rpx units, touch-first
- Warm and encouraging, not clinical or corporate
- Avoid: dark mode glassmorphism, cold blues, anything that looks corporate/enterprise

### Task Category Color System
- academic (学业类 📚) → #5eaeff (blue)
- sports (运动类 🏃) → #06d6a0 (green)
- language (语言学习 🌍) → #ffb627 (amber)
- art (艺术类 🎨) → #c77dff (purple)
- behavior (行为习惯 🌟) → #ff6b9d (pink)

### Design Principles
1. **Reward visibility** — Gold, XP, and stars are the core motivation loop; make reward display feel special and distinct
2. **Category scanability** — 5 task categories must be visually distinguishable at a glance via consistent color coding
3. **Warmth over precision** — Slightly warm grays, soft gradients, rounded corners; nothing clinical
4. **Status clarity** — Progress states (pending/submitted/approved/rejected) must be unmistakable, especially in week navigation
5. **Child-appropriate** — Large touch targets, emoji-forward, encouraging copy; never intimidating
